About Sam

Born April 22, 1985, Sam Altman is the CEO of OpenAI and a leading figure in the AI boom. His journey in technology began early in his life in St. Louis, where he developed a fascination with computers from the age of eight.

Sam attended Stanford University to study computer science but chose to drop out to pursue his entrepreneurial ambitions. At just 19 years old, he co-founded Loopt, a location-based social networking app, marking the beginning of his impressive career in tech innovation.

In 2014, Sam became president of Y Combinator, where he significantly scaled its operations and impact in the startup ecosystem. Under his leadership, Y Combinator's portfolio value grew to over $100 billion, supporting now-household names like Airbnb, Dropbox, and Reddit.

In 2015, Sam co-founded OpenAI with the mission to ensure that artificial general intelligence benefits all of humanity. Now as CEO, he leads the organization in developing groundbreaking AI models that are transforming how we interact with technology.
